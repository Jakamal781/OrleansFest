Let the Good Times Roll

Your 2016 Summer Adventure

Total Rewards® and Harrah's New Orleans invite you to discover one of the world's most fascinating cities. New Orleans is a truly unique melting pot of culture, food and music. Harrah's offers luxurious accommodations and over 100,000 square feet of gaming in the heart of the city, steps away from the French Quarter. With the exciting offers and recommended activities enclosed, you're guaranteed to have a good time in The Big Easy when you come to stay.

The French Quarter


The French Quarter, also known as the Vieux Carré, is the oldest neighborhood in the city of New Orleans. With so much culture, history and entertainment, you'll find an exciting experience around every corner.

Take in the history of the French Quarter through an enchanting carriage ride. Marvel at the architectural gems as you stroll down Royal Street browsing the treasure trove of galleries, antique shops and boutiques.

Stroll alongside the Mississippi River and pop in to Jackson Square. Peruse the French Market, a historically charming open-air market featuring five blocks of local produce, specialty art, handmade crafts, shopping and more.

Seeking Live Jazz? Fritzel's European Jazz Club on Bourbon Street is a mecca for Jazz lovers and musicians alike. Whether you want to play or just listen, there is always a warm welcome awaiting you there.

World-Class Eats

Commander's Palace

Travel down St. Charles Avenue to this New Orleans landmark. Modern cuisine meets Creole classics inside a historic Garden District mansion. Come see why Zagat has voted it the most popular restaurant in town 18 years in a row. A favorite among locals is the lively jazz brunch where you can enjoy sparkling cocktails and the classic turtle soup.

Besh Steak

Harrah's New Orleans is home to James Beard Award winner, Chef John Besh. Experience his love of local ingredients and cooking techniques with traditional steakhouse favorites. Enjoy the rich flavors of barbecued shrimp or a tender 22 oz. Porterhouse. Besh Steak also impresses with hand-selected wine and spirits to accompany each dish.

Central Grocery

No trip to The Big Easy is complete without a visit to Central Grocery on Decatur Street in the French Quarter. This Italian-American grocery serves an original New Orleans staple - the muffuletta. This oversized sandwich features toasted bread, marinated olive salad and Italian meats and cheeses.

Acme Oyster House

Since 1910 Acme Oyster House has been serving New Orleans classics including legendary po'boys, seafood gumbo and jambalaya. With several locations in the city including the French Quarter and inside Harrah's New Orleans, it's easy to experience this longtime New Orleans favorite.

Take Fun Indoors

Audubon Aquarium of the Americas

Just steps from Harrah's Casino is the world-renowned Audubon Aquarium. Get up close (and eye-to-eye if you please) with some of the most fascinating oceanic creatures. Look for the rare and endangered white alligator. In Cajun lore, seeing a white alligator is considered good luck.

The National World War II Museum

A must-see for history lovers and all patriots, The National World War II Museum has been designated by the U.S. Congress as America's official WWII Museum. From the 1930s prelude to war to the Normandy Invasion and the battles of the Pacific Islands, visitors trace America's role in the war and on the home front.

Steamboat Natchez

Relish the beauty and romance of the New Orleans skyline from aboard the last authentic steamboat on the Mississippi River. Choose from a two-hour daytime cruise, Sunday jazz brunch cruise or nighttime dinner cruise.

Old U.S. Mint

Situated at the foot of Esplanade Avenue is the Old U.S. Mint. Built in 1835 and used as a minting facility until 1909, the museum is now home to a number of stunning permanent exhibits including a New Orleans Jazz exhibit, historical photography and several rotating exhibits.

Must-Do Festivals

Tales of the Cocktail | July 19 - 24

What started as a small gathering of cocktail lovers has grown into the nation's premier cocktail festival with seminars, tastings, mixology competitions and much more.

Satchmo SummerFest | August 5 - 7

Paying homage to Louis "Satchmo" Armstrong and the vital role he played in the development of American Jazz, Satchmo SummerFest has become a favorite festival for tourists and locals alike. The three-day festival features outdoor concerts, music history seminars, jazz exhibits, a jazz mass, a second-line parade and plenty of local food.

Southern Decadence | August 31 - September 5

Held annually over Labor Day weekend, this celebration of LGBT culture attracts over 125,000 participants from all over the world. The entertainment lineup includes parades, drag shows, contests, DJs, music and other events.
